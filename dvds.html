"Billy Bounce"


After presenting the classic routines of Eddie Brown, Coles & Atkins and Stan Kahn, Sam Weber steps out on his own with his choreography to Charlie Parker's Bebop classic.

"Eddie Brown's "E.B.Choruses"


Tap master Eddie Brown was an inspirational example and mentor to generations of dancers. His "E.B. Choruses" is a masterpiece of swing and syncopation, essential to any tap dancer's education. Sam Weber analyzes the musical form of each step cataloguing the different elements, weight changes and rhythms.

"Stan Kahn's "EE""


Stan Kahn and Louis DaPron were lifelong friends. Stan, whose style was very airborne, was influenced by Louis, who favored a more close-to-the-floor "hoofing" style, with thirds, cramprolls and slides. One of Stan's most advanced routines, "EE" pays tribute to the influence of his friend, Louis DaPron.

"Music for Tap CDs"


Sam Weber's "Music For Tap" is a four-CD set of jazz standards for use in teaching, improvising or performing. Selections range from 32-bar standards, blues and bebop to Latin and Funk, slow to fast tempos, with time signatures in 4, 3, 6, 5, and 7. Many tunes feature open breaks and stop time. Great for practicing.
